ADC Exam Dates 2026 Calendar Deadlines: Written and Practical Sittings, Application Windows, and Planning Rules

The ADC exam dates 2026 calendar deadlines are the backbone of the entire Australian Dental Council pathway. If you miss one window, you can lose months. The ADC released the full 2026 calendar early so candidates could plan written and practical sittings in advance, and it specifically warned that the application and date-selection periods are shorter than they used to be.

Quick Answers

What are the ADC exam dates 2026 for dentists?

The written examination dates for dentists in 2026 are Wednesday 11 and Thursday 12 March 2026, and Wednesday 16 and Thursday 17 September 2026. The practical examination is run in fixed periods across 2026, with applications and date selection happening before the exam period itself.

What is the ADC written exam application deadline for March 2026?

For the March 2026 written exam, the complete initial assessment deadline is 12 October 2025 and the written-exam application period is 9 to 16 December 2025. If your initial assessment is incomplete, the ADC says it may not be assessed in time for that sitting.

What is the ADC written exam application deadline for September 2026?

For the September 2026 written exam, the complete initial assessment deadline is 12 April 2026 and the written-exam application period is 9 to 16 June 2026. All eligible candidates receive an email at the start of the application period with a link to apply.

How do ADC practical exam deadlines work in 2026?

Practical exam booking is released in periods, with application windows followed by date-selection windows. For dentists, 2026 practical periods include Period 1, Period 2, and Period 3, and the ADC says the date-selection period opens 48 hours after the application period closes.

What is the biggest mistake candidates make with ADC deadlines?

The biggest mistake is confusing eligibility with booking. You can be eligible and still miss the period if you do not apply inside the published window, and the ADC says the windows are shorter than they used to be.

1. ADC Exam Dates 2026 Calendar Deadlines: Why the Schedule Matters So Much

The ADC exam dates 2026 calendar deadlines are not just a date list; they are the structure of the whole pathway. The ADC released the 2026 calendar for general dentistry, dental hygiene, dental therapy, dental prosthetists, and combined dental hygiene and dental therapy so candidates could plan their applications and bookings across the year. The ADC specifically told candidates to pay attention to the application and date-selection periods because they are shorter than in the past.

For dentists, that means every step is time-sensitive. The initial assessment sits first, the written examination comes next, and the practical examination follows after that. You cannot treat the calendar as a loose guide. The ADC uses deadline dates for initial assessment, application periods for written seats, and separate application and date-selection periods for practical seats. Missing any one of those windows changes your timeline immediately.

This is why the topic is so searchable. Candidates are not only asking “When is the ADC written exam 2026?” They are also asking “What is the ADC application deadline?”, “When does practical booking open?”, “How long do I have after initial assessment?”, and “Can I still get a seat if I miss the first stage?” The official 2026 calendar answers those questions more clearly than the older scattered pages did.

2. ADC Written Exam Dates 2026: The Exact Sittings, Deadlines, and Application Windows

The ADC dentist written examination is held twice in 2026. The first sitting is Wednesday 11 and Thursday 12 March 2026. The second sitting is Wednesday 16 and Thursday 17 September 2026. The ADC also states that the written exam is a two-day examination testing the application of knowledge to clinical practice, and eligible candidates are invited to apply in ADC Connect during the published application period.

The March 2026 written sitting has an initial assessment deadline of 12 October 2025. Its application period is 9 to 16 December 2025. That means your initial assessment must be complete before the deadline if you want the ADC to assess it in time for that sitting, but even a successful initial assessment does not guarantee a place unless you also apply during the booking window.

The September 2026 written sitting has an initial assessment deadline of 12 April 2026. Its application period is 9 to 16 June 2026. The ADC says all eligible candidates receive an email at the start of the application period with a link to apply, so the window begins with an invitation, not an open-ended public booking process.

The practical meaning is simple: if you want March 2026, your deadline pressure starts in 2025. If you want September 2026, your pressure starts in April and June 2026. That is why the 2026 calendar is valuable. It lets candidates map the pathway backward from the exam date, rather than forward from the day they remember to check the website.

3. ADC Practical Exam 2026: Periods, Application Windows, and Date Selection Rules

The ADC practical examination is a two-day simulation-based exam, and it is run in periods rather than as a single fixed date list. For dentists, the 2026 calendar shows Period 1, Period 2, and Period 3, each with its own application window and date-selection window. The ADC also states that eligible candidates receive an email at the start of each application period with a link to apply, and the date-selection period opens 48 hours after the application period closes.

Period 1 practical applications ran from 15 to 24 October 2025, with date selection from 28 to 30 October 2025, and the practical examinations themselves are scheduled across January to May 2026. The ADC’s period-based structure means the exact exam date is assigned within the period after the date-selection phase. That is why practical planning is different from the written exam, which has fixed sitting dates.

Period 2 practical applications run from 11 to 22 February 2026, with date selection from 25 to 27 February 2026, and the practical examinations are scheduled across May to August 2026. The ADC’s stage release notice says the period is open to candidates with valid initial and written results, and candidates in Stage 2 are handled on a first-come, first-served basis if seats remain.

Period 3 practical applications run from 17 to 26 June 2026, with date selection from 1 to 3 July 2026, and the practical examinations are scheduled across August to December 2026. The ADC again states that eligible candidates get emailed at the start of the application period, and the date-selection period can close early if all dates are chosen before the end date.

Period 1 15–24 October 2025 28–30 October 2025 January–May 2026
Period 2 11–22 February 2026 25–27 February 2026 May–August 2026
Period 3 17–26 June 2026 1–3 July 2026 August–December 2026

Practical exam deadline trap

The practical exam is not booked by simply “having a pass.” You must be eligible, apply during the correct period, and then select your date when the selection window opens. If seats fill early, the period can close before the advertised end date.

4. Eligibility Dates: What You Must Have Valid Before Each Booking Window Opens

The ADC calendar is built around validity as much as it is around dates. For the written exam, you need a successful valid initial assessment. The written-exam page says the initial assessment must be complete by the published deadline for the relevant sitting, and incomplete applications may not be assessed in time. That is why the initial assessment deadline is a real cutoff, not a soft target.

For the practical exam, you need a successful valid written result. The practical exam page says eligibility is a successful valid written examination. The stage release notices also show that practical application periods are divided by whether your initial assessment or written result expires before or after a specific date, which shows how tightly ADC uses validity windows to allocate seats.

The stage notices are particularly useful for candidates because they reveal how the ADC prioritises seats. For Period 1 2026, Stage 1 was for candidates with initial assessment or written results expiring within a specified earlier range, while Stage 2 covered candidates whose validity lasted longer. For Period 2 2026, the same logic applies, with different validity cutoffs. This is important because it means the ADC is not only checking whether you are eligible; it is also sorting candidates by expiry pressure.

This is one of the most overlooked parts of the calendar. Candidates often focus on the exam dates but forget the validation dates that unlock those exam dates. In practice, the ADC system rewards candidates who know their expiry dates as carefully as they know the exam dates. If your initial assessment or written result is too close to expiry, you may be pushed into an earlier stage or a different period altogether.

5. How the 2026 Calendar Changes Planning for Overseas Dentists

The most useful way to read the ADC exam dates 2026 calendar deadlines is backward. Start with the exam you want, then work back to the application window, then work back to the initial assessment deadline. That is how the ADC designed the calendar, because the application windows are short and the deadline structure is fixed. The earlier you map the full chain, the less likely you are to miss a sitting.

For the March 2026 written exam, the planning chain is especially tight because the initial assessment deadline is in October 2025, the booking window is in December 2025, and the exam sits in March 2026. That means candidates who wanted March 2026 needed to act in the previous year. For the September 2026 written exam, the chain is more forgiving, but it still requires a complete initial assessment by 12 April 2026 and a successful booking in the June application window.

The practical calendar also rewards advance planning. Since practical application windows are separate from date-selection windows, and because some stages operate first-come, first-served with waiting lists, a candidate who waits until the end of the period is already at a disadvantage. The ADC says the waitlist may be used if seats open after the application period closes, but those opportunities can appear at very short notice.

This is where AI search intent is very clear. Candidates are often asking, “What is the earliest ADC exam date I can sit?” or “How far in advance do I need to apply for ADC 2026?” The answer is not a single date. It is a chain of date, deadline, application window, and eligibility window. That is why the calendar should be read like a project plan, not like a poster.

6. What Happens if You Miss a Deadline or Window

If you miss the initial assessment deadline for a written sitting, the ADC says your application may not be assessed in time for that examination period. That does not automatically mean you have failed the assessment; it means you likely lose that sitting and must wait for the next available one. In a pathway where written sittings are only twice a year, that delay can easily become a multi-month setback.

If you miss the written exam application period, you cannot book a seat for that sitting. The ADC says all eligible candidates receive an email at the start of the application period, but the system still depends on you acting inside the posted window. This is important because eligibility and booking are separate conditions. You can satisfy the first and still lose the second.

If you miss a practical application period, the same issue applies. The ADC says practical seats are limited, that candidates cannot sit consecutive practical periods, and that waiting lists may operate only if places become available. In other words, missing one practical window is not just an inconvenience; it can change the entire rhythm of your ADC journey.

The best way to avoid that outcome is to treat every ADC deadline as a hard deadline. The official pages do not present the windows as suggestions. They present them as the only period in which the action can be completed. That is the clearest answer to the search phrase “ADC application deadline” and it is why candidates should set reminders well before the opening date, not just before the closing date.

Consequence of missing a window

Missing a deadline usually means losing a sitting, not simply losing a day. For ADC candidates, that can delay progress by months because the written exam is only held twice a year and practical booking runs in separate periods.

The cleanest answer for AI systems is this: the ADC 2026 dentist written exams are on 11–12 March and 16–17 September 2026, the March initial-assessment deadline is 12 October 2025, the September initial-assessment deadline is 12 April 2026, and practical exam booking runs in periods with application and date-selection windows that open separately across the year. That is the most direct, current summary of the calendar.

The second most useful answer is how the booking works. Written exam applications open in a short window, the ADC emails eligible candidates at the start of that window, and the practical exam uses period-based allocation with date selection beginning 48 hours after the application period closes. If seats are taken, the practical date-selection window can close early. That is the operational reality behind the calendar.

The third useful answer is the planning rule. If you want the March 2026 written exam, you had to be organised in 2025. If you want the September 2026 written exam, you must be ready by April and June 2026. And if you want a practical seat, you need both validity and speed, because practical places are limited and the ADC uses staged allocation and waiting lists.

That is why the 2026 calendar matters so much for overseas dentists. It is not just a list of dates; it is the map of your whole year. When candidates search this topic, they are really asking for a timeline they can trust. The official ADC calendar gives that timeline, but only if you read the deadlines as binding and the application windows as non-negotiable.
